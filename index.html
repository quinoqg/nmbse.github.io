Natural Materials and Biomimetics for Sustainable Engineering

📅 Wednesday, 17 June 2026 📍 Imperial College London, South Kensington Campus

About the Workshop

This workshop will bring together researchers across Europe to explore the role of natural and bio-based materials in engineering applications. The focus will be on natural fibre composites, bio-derived materials, and biomimetic strategies as sustainable alternatives to carbon-intensive materials used in infrastructure, transport, and energy systems. Presentations will be delivered by invited researchers and expert speakers from across Europe working on these themes. The workshop is open to the wider community for attendance, free of charge, either in person at Imperial College London or online via Cassyni livestream, but registration is required in both cases.

Key Themes

  • Natural fibre composites — processing, characterisation, and performance
  • Bio-derived and bio-inspired materials — from molecular design to structural applications
  • Biomimetic engineering — learning from biological structures and systems
  • Sustainability and circular economy — life-cycle assessment, recyclability, and green manufacturing

Venue

The workshop will be held at Imperial College London, South Kensington Campus, Exhibition Road, London SW7 2AZ. Attendance will be hybrid, anyone interested will be able to join online.
